## 01 · Lloyds Banking Group — AI-enabled lending platform

> Positioning line: he takes broken, political environments and ships responsible AI
> that makes money, and the team is better for it.

### The Challenge
I inherited a key strategic outcome on Lloyds' Commercial Lending Platform, leading a
cross-functional team of around 80 across product, engineering, design and data. The
brief was hard enough on its own: build the bank's first automated SME lending
decisioning, and a GenAI financial-spreading product, both visible at COO level. The
environment was harder. Platform teams were dysfunctional and openly hostile to each
other, dates were dictated top down, leadership was mis-joined, and priorities
conflicted week to week.

### Approach
I had two problems to solve at once, delivery and people. On delivery, I chose to build
the SME decisioning capability incrementally rather than as a big-bang launch, so real
value reached customers early and we could learn while we built. On the GenAI product, I
insisted on a robust human-in-the-loop workflow and a proper evaluation harness before
we scaled anything, so the output could be trusted. Underneath both, I worked to turn
the hostility between platform teams into a group that actually delivered together.

### What We Built
The bank's first automated SME decisioning workflow, shipped in increments so lending
teams saw value from month one. Alongside it, a GenAI financial-spreading product with
human-in-the-loop review and evaluations built in from the start, not bolted on later.

### Outcomes
The automated SME decisioning workflow generated **£5.8M in its first month live**,
measured on launch-month data and cleared by the team to share. Delivery was recognised
at COO level. And the teams that started out hostile to each other now work together
well, which I am as proud of as the number.

### Learnings
In a top-down, politically charged environment, your delivery strategy is also a
leadership tool. Shipping incrementally bought trust that dictated dates never could.
And with GenAI, the discipline of evals and human-in-the-loop before scaling is not a
brake on delivery, it is the thing that makes the value safe to claim.

## 02 · HCA Healthcare · Primary Care — private healthcare's digital front door

### The Challenge
HCA's private primary-care booking ran on a third-party practice-management system. It
did not match their recent rebrand, and it did not match the standard of care patients
got inside the clinic. Under 30% of bookings were happening online. Before HCA invested
in marketing and scaled the business, they needed to own their digital front door and
make it feel as premium as the service behind it.

### Approach
I ran end-to-end patient discovery, researching directly with patients to find the real
friction in the acquisition funnel rather than guessing at it internally. From there I
owned the roadmap from zero to one across both web and a native app, and reimagined the
online booking journey so it mirrored HCA's premium in-clinic experience.

### What We Built
A new native app and web experience for booking GP appointments and health screening,
managing results, and handling subscriptions. The app and web were built on a shared
component framework so both platforms stayed consistent and could evolve together. We
launched online booking for health screenings as part of it.

### Outcomes
Online bookings grew **+41%**, from under 30% to nearly half of all primary care
bookings. The work unlocked **£6M+ in screening revenue** and now handles around **73k
online bookings a year**.

### Learnings
In premium healthcare, the digital experience has to match the clinical one, or it
quietly erodes trust in the brand. Anchoring the roadmap on funnel friction I had
actually researched with patients, rather than internal assumptions, is what moved the
booking numbers.

## 03 · Wagamama · Soul Club — a loyalty product a million people actually use

### The Challenge
Wagamama had no loyalty scheme while its rivals did. They needed to lift visit frequency
and build a relationship with guests beyond the four walls of the restaurant, but there
was no proven model for what loyalty should even mean for a brand built on food and
hospitality.

### Approach
I started with discovery to set the loyalty strategy, rather than jumping straight to
features. We ran dual-track agile with heavy user research, and chose a flexible content
platform so the product could keep evolving after launch. The key insight was to design
the loyalty loop around the real in-restaurant moment: check in at your table, earn a
stamp, redeem a reward, all while you are actually there.

### What We Built
Soul Club: a check-in flow tied to your restaurant and table, a stamp-based rewards
engine with redemption and minimum-spend logic, a content-led feed of recipes, events
and editorial, and an ordering and split-bill flow. The app launched around nine months
after build began.

### Outcomes
Soul Club launched at **№1 on the iOS App Store for Food & Drink**. Adoption beat
projections by **300%**, the app passed **a million users**, and floor staff folded
check-in into the way they welcomed guests to the table.

### Learnings
Loyalty is not points, it is a reason to come back. The content feed drove incremental
visits as much as the rewards did. And designing for the physical table moment, instead
of a generic loyalty app, is what made people actually use it.
